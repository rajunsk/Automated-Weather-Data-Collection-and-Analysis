# Temperature Analysis (2015–2026)

## Overview
This analysis explores temperature behavior from **2015–2026** using multiple visualizations.  
The goal is to understand **distribution, seasonality, variability, long-term trends, and relationships between temperature variables**, while highlighting **seasonal climatic effects**.

---

## 1. Temperature Distribution
The distribution of daily mean temperature follows an **approximately normal distribution** centered around **25–26°C**.

- Mean temperature ≈ **25.8°C**
- Median temperature ≈ **25.4°C**

Because the mean and median are close, the data shows **minimal skewness**.  
The Q-Q plot confirms that temperature values closely follow a **normal statistical distribution**, with only slight deviations at extreme values.

---

## 2. Seasonal Temperature Patterns
Monthly climatology reveals a **strong seasonal cycle**.

Temperature progression during a typical year:

| Season | Months | Temperature Behavior |
|------|------|------|
| Winter | Dec – Feb | Coolest period of the year |
| Summer | Mar – May | Rapid warming and peak temperatures |
| Monsoon | Jun – Sep | Gradual cooling and stabilization |
| Autumn | Oct – Nov | Transition back toward winter |

Temperatures generally:

- Rise from **January → May**
- Peak during **April–May**
- Decrease during **June–September**
- Continue cooling into **winter months**

This repeating pattern confirms **stable regional climatic seasonality**.

---

## 3. Daily Temperature Range and Seasonal Effects
The heatmap of **daily temperature range (Max − Min)** shows strong seasonal influences.

### Winter Effect
Winter months show **larger daily temperature ranges (≈11–14°C)**.

Reasons:
- Clear skies
- Strong nighttime cooling
- Larger difference between day and night temperatures

### Summer Effect
Summer still shows **relatively large ranges (≈10–12°C)** due to intense daytime heating.

### Monsoon Effect
Monsoon months show **smallest temperature ranges (≈5–8°C)**.

Reasons:
- High humidity
- Cloud cover
- Rainfall limiting daytime heating
- Reduced nighttime cooling

Thus, monsoon acts as a **temperature stabilizer**.

---

## 4. Seasonal Temperature Distributions
Seasonal distribution plots confirm clear differences between seasons.

### Summer
- Highest temperatures (≈28–33°C)
- Greater variability
- Extreme heat days possible

### Monsoon
- Moderate temperatures (≈25–27°C)
- More stable climate conditions
- Reduced daily fluctuations

### Autumn
- Gradual cooling phase
- Temperatures around **24–26°C**

### Winter
- Lowest temperatures (≈21–24°C)
- Cooler nights and mild days

These seasonal distributions clearly separate the **four climatic regimes**.

---

## 5. Long-Term Temperature Trends
The rolling-average time series reveals both **short-term and long-term behavior**.

### 30-Day Rolling Mean
- Highlights **monthly seasonal cycles**
- Shows warming in summer and cooling in winter.

### 365-Day Rolling Mean
- Represents the **long-term annual trend**
- Remains stable around **25–26°C**.

There is **no strong long-term warming or cooling trend** within this time period.

---

## 6. Year-to-Year Temperature Consistency
The year-over-year monthly comparison shows:

- All years follow a **similar seasonal curve**.
- Peak temperatures consistently occur during **April–May**.
- Winter temperatures remain the **lowest across all years**.

Although minor differences occur between years, the **overall climate pattern remains stable**.

---

## 7. Temperature Anomalies
Temperature anomaly analysis measures deviations from the **long-term monthly average**.

- **Positive anomalies:** Warmer than average months
- **Negative anomalies:** Cooler than average months

Most anomalies fall within **±2°C**, indicating moderate variability without extreme climatic shifts.

This suggests **natural climate variability rather than major long-term climate change** in the dataset.

---

## 8. Relationship Between Minimum and Maximum Temperature
The scatter plot of **daily minimum vs maximum temperature** shows a strong positive relationship.

Seasonal clustering appears:

- **Summer cluster:** High minimum and maximum temperatures
- **Monsoon cluster:** Moderate temperatures
- **Winter cluster:** Lower temperature values

This demonstrates how **seasonal climate affects daily temperature ranges**.

---

## 9. Regression Analysis
Regression between **mean temperature and maximum temperature** shows:

- Correlation coefficient: **r ≈ 0.91**
- Indicates a **very strong linear relationship**.

Residual analysis shows most errors centered around zero, confirming the regression captures the **core temperature relationship effectively**.

---

# Final Insights
From the complete analysis:

- The region exhibits a **strong and consistent seasonal temperature cycle**.
- **Summer produces the highest temperatures**, while **winter remains the coolest period**.
- **Monsoon season stabilizes temperature fluctuations** due to clouds and rainfall.
- Daily temperature relationships remain **stable across years**.
- Mean temperature strongly predicts maximum temperature.
- Long-term temperature levels remain **relatively stable over the study period**.

---

The dataset demonstrates **clear seasonal climatic behavior with predictable temperature cycles**.  
Seasonal effects such as **summer heating, monsoon stabilization, and winter cooling** strongly influence daily and monthly temperature patterns.

These insights make the dataset suitable for:
